How to Reduce OT Cancellations by 80% — A Step-by-Step Guide for Surgery Coordinators

Operations • Apr 2026 • 7 min read

Every time a scheduled surgery is cancelled on the day of the procedure, a hospital loses between ₹50,000 to ₹2,000,000 in unutilized OT time, wasted sterilized consumables, and idle staff hours. We analyzed over 12,000 cancelled surgeries and found that 80% of them are entirely preventable.

The Top Causes of OT Cancellations

When we audited hospital operations, we found three recurring culprits:

1. Missing Pre-Op Clearances (PAC)

The number one reason for cancellation: The patient arrives at the OT, but the PAC (Pre-Anesthesia Checkup) or mandatory blood work is incomplete or the results are missing. The anesthesiologist rightly refuses to proceed.

2. Surgeon or Staff Unavailability

A surgeon is stuck in an emergency, or the specialized scrub nurse was accidentally rostered for a different ward. Relying on WhatsApp groups and whiteboards for scheduling almost guarantees these clashes.

3. Equipment Shortages

The surgery requires a specific C-arm or a specialized surgical drill, but it is currently undergoing maintenance or is locked in another OT.

The Smart Fix: Integrated OT Management

A modern OT Management Software acts as an air traffic control system for your surgical suites.

  • Automated Checklists: The system locks the OT booking until all pre-op checklists (PAC, consents, blood typing) are digitally signed off.
  • Conflict-Free Scheduling: The software checks the real-time roster of surgeons, anesthetists, and scrub nurses before confirming a slot.
  • Asset Tracking: It ensures that the required medical equipment is reserved and confirmed working.

By implementing a unified system, hospitals can reclaim lost OT revenue and dramatically improve surgical outcomes.
