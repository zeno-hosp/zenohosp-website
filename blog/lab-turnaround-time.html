Why Your Lab's Turnaround Time Is Killing Patient Trust - And the One Change That Fixes It

Technology • Mar 2026 • 5 min read

When a patient leaves the phlebotomy chair, a mental countdown begins. Every hour they wait for their report increases their anxiety. In the modern healthcare landscape, Turnaround Time (TAT) is the ultimate metric of patient satisfaction.

Where Do the Hours Go?

Many hospital administrators mistakenly believe the analyzer machines are slow. In reality, the actual testing process is incredibly fast. The delays happen before the blood goes in and after the results come out.

1. Manual Accessioning & Barcoding

If a technician has to manually type patient details from a paper requisition slip into the analyzer, you lose precious minutes and invite critical typographical errors.

2. Transcribing Results

The analyzer prints a physical receipt. A data entry operator types these numbers into the hospital's billing system so the doctor can see them. This dual-entry process is a massive bottleneck.

3. The Physical Signature

The report sits on a desk waiting for the Chief Pathologist to physically review and sign it.

The LIS Integration Fix

By implementing a modern Laboratory Information System (LIS) with bi-directional analyzer integration, you eliminate all three bottlenecks instantly.

With ZenoHosp LIS, the HMS sends the test order directly to the barcode printer. The analyzer reads the barcode, runs the test, and automatically pushes the digital result back into the patient's EMR. The pathologist reviews and signs electronically, and an SMS with a secure download link is instantly sent to the patient. A 12-hour process becomes a 2-hour process.
