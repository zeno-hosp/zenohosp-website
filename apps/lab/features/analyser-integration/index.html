How the Interface Works

Sample barcode sent to analyser. Result received back. Automatically.

When a sample is collected and registered in ZenoHosp, the sample barcode and test order are sent to the connected analyser via the bidirectional interface. The analyser processes the sample. When analysis is complete, results are sent back to ZenoHosp and posted to the patient's record — no manual entry at any step.

  • Sample barcode and test order sent to analyser on collection
  • Analyser processes sample using received order
  • Results returned to ZenoHosp when analysis complete
  • Results posted to patient record — technician reviews before sign-off

Supported Analysers

HL7 and ASTM cover the major analyser brands used in Indian hospitals.

ZenoHosp's lab interface supports HL7 (LIS-2) and ASTM protocols, which cover the majority of analysers used in Indian hospitals. Haematology, biochemistry, immunology, and microbiology analysers from major manufacturers are supported. Both unidirectional and bidirectional modes are available.

  • Sysmex (haematology): XN series, XS series
  • Roche Cobas (biochemistry and immunology): c311, e411, e601
  • Mindray (haematology and biochemistry): BC series, BS series
  • Abbott Architect (immunology and chemistry)
  • BioMérieux Vitek 2 (microbiology and blood culture)

Frequently asked questions

Which analyser brands does ZenoHosp support?

ZenoHosp supports HL7 and ASTM protocols, covering Sysmex, Roche Cobas, Mindray, Abbott Architect, and BioMérieux Vitek 2, among others. For specific model compatibility, contact our lab integration team.

How does bidirectional integration reduce transcription errors?

With bidirectional integration, results are transmitted electronically from the analyser to ZenoHosp. There is no manual transcription of values. The technician reviews the electronically received result and confirms — no typing involved.

Does ZenoHosp support unidirectional connections?

Yes. For analysers that support only result transmission (not order receipt), ZenoHosp supports unidirectional integration. Results are received and posted automatically; orders are entered manually on the analyser side.
